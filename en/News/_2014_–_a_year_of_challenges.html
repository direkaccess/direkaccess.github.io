2014 – a year of challenges at VTB Bank (Austria) AG

Vienna, 29th April 2015 - The past fiscal year 2014 was affected by challenging, economic and political conditions. VTB Bank (Austria) AG was still able to keep its traditional corporate lending business and its international customer relationships stable in 2014. With the 40th anniversary VTB Bank (Austria) AG furthermore underlines its consistency in the Austrian banking market.

2014 – even at the 40th anniversary of the founding, the challenges remain

VTB Bank (Austria) AG is one of the most important European partner banks for the structuring and financing of trade relationships and projects between East and West.

2014 was marked by international tensions and an unsettled situation in the securities markets. "In particular, the movements on the international markets with regard to bond prices and exchange rates have significantly affected our results. Because of the consolidation of the portfolio of VTB Bank (Austria) AG starting in fiscal year 2014, we were able to continue to ensure the quality in terms of risks and return," comments Alexey Krokhin, CEO of VTB Bank (Austria) AG.

VTB Bank (Austria) AG closed 2014 with EUR 9.6 billion in consolidated total assets. The eligible own funds amounted to EUR 1.47 billion, which corresponds to a common equity tier 1 (CET1) of 9.3%.

2014 was a very special year for VTB Bank (Austria) AG, as it celebrated its 40th anniversary on the Austrian banking market. Mr. Krokhin summarizes the company history: "We started in 1974 as a small financial institution and are now established as an internationally successful company. We are very proud to look back on years of success and are confident to continue our success story in the upcoming years. Over the past 40 years challenging times have often been there, but we have always been able to cope well." Mr. Krokhin hint at the new difficulties for the banking sector the year 2014 brought. Regulatory requirements have been reinforced, and additional monetary and fiscal policies have been issued. Those need to be further conquered in 2015, considering that cross-border acting is thus made not any easier. “Nevertheless, it will be possible to fully provide all banking services of VTB Bank (Austria) AG to its business partners. Because, we continue seeing it as our mission to support our customers in the implementation of their plans the best possible." Mr. Krokhin promises in conclusion.

VTB Direktbank: successfully expanded market positioning in 2014

Since March 2011, VTB Bank (Austria) AG has been represented on the German market by its branch office VTB Direktbank. Based in Frankfurt am Main, VTB Direktbank appeals online to German retail customers with market-oriented savings and investment products. In 2014, the direct bank strengthened its position in the German market. 3 new products have been introduced. In addition, the direct bank has received numerous awards for outstanding products and services. Despite current turbulent times, the acceptance of VTB Direktbank on the German market is high and the trust of customers unbroken.

About VTB Bank (Austria) AG

VTB Bank (Austria) AG is one of the largest foreign banks in Austria. The European Subholding of Russian VTB Bank is a commercial bank with EUR 9.6 billion in consolidated total assets and EUR 1.47 billion in eligible own funds. VTB Bank (Austria) AG is specialised in the financing of trade transactions and projects between East and West, as well as in services in the areas of payments and investments. The Austrian operations as well as the subsidiaries VTB Bank (Deutschland) AG and VTB Bank (France) SA are combined under VTB Bank (Austria) AG. VTB Direktbank, an online bank active in Germany, offers top terms for overnight and fixed term deposit accounts, is a branch office of VTB Bank (Austria) AG.
