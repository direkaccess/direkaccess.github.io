2013 – a year characterised by the stabilisation of VTB Bank (Austria) AG

Vienna, 28 April 2014 - Despite a continued challenging economic environment, VTB Bank (Austria) AG managed to maintain the stability of its traditional corporate lending business in Russia and the CIS countries as well as its customer relationships in Western Europe in 2013. In the new financial year it will focus on long-term internal restructuring processes as well as steps to further harmonise the European Sub-Holding banks Austria, Germany and France.

 

2013 - a challenging year for VTB Bank (Austria) AG

VTB Bank (Austria) AG is one of the most important European partner banks for the structuring and financing of trade relationships and projects with Russia and the CIS countries. The Russian subsidiary has recorded consistent growth since 2008.

In 2013, the financial and capital markets were characterised by a changing situation. "As a result of necessary loan write-offs and valuation allowances, we were unable to further expand our net profit for the financial year for the first time in years. Nevertheless, the creditworthiness of our loan portfolio remains satisfactory", commented Alexey Krokhin, Chairman of the Management Board of VTB Bank (Austria) AG. With respect to repo transactions, the investment banking business was even significantly expanded. VTB Bank (Austria) AG closed 2013 with EUR 10.3 billion in consolidated total assets. The eligible own funds as at the balance sheet date amount to EUR 1.37 billion, which corresponds to an equity ratio of 7.6% (Tier 1) and 15.2% (Tier 2).

 

2014 - VTB Bank (Austria) AG continues internal process of harmonisation

VTB Bank (Austria) AG is focusing on the internal restructuring process of its core banking system in 2014. "This is a promising long-term project that will lead to increased efficiency and improved effectiveness in processing our customer transactions", explains Krokhin. VTB Bank (Austria) AG also continues to focus on the growth of its staff and plans to expand the number of employees by the end of the year. The consolidation initiated in 2012 will also be further pursued. "The harmonisation of the European Sub-Holding banks Austria, Germany and France has proceeded satisfactorily thus far and will remain in our focus in order to make the best possible use of the synergy potential within the Group", explains Krokhin.

In addition, 2014 marks VTB Bank (Austria) AG's 40th year of conducting business in Vienna.

 

VTB Direktbank Deutschland: market presence maintained in 2013

Since March 2011, VTB Bank (Austria) AG has been represented on the German market by its branch office VTB Direktbank. Based in Frankfurt am Main, Direktbank appeals to retail customers with market-oriented savings and investment products. In 2013, Direktbank succeeded in maintaining its positioning in the German market. "In the coming year we expect increasing competition in the direct and online banking market, which we will successfully counter by continuing to systematically develop our product portfolio", says Krokhin regarding the future of VTB Direktbank, which is based in Germany.

 

About VTB Bank (Austria) AG

VTB Bank (Austria) AG is one of the largest foreign banks in Austria. The European sub-holding of Russian VTB Bank is a traditional commercial bank with EUR 10.3 billion in consolidated total assets and EUR 1.37 billion in eligible own funds. VTB Bank (Austria) AG specialises in the financing of trade transactions and projects in Russia and the CIS countries as well as in services in the areas of payment transactions and investments. The Austrian operations as well as the subsidiaries VTB Bank (Deutschland) AG and VTB Bank (France) SA are combined under VTB Bank (Austria) AG. VTB Direktbank, which is an online bank active in Germany that offers top terms for overnight and fixed term deposit accounts, is a branch office of VTB Bank (Austria) AG.
