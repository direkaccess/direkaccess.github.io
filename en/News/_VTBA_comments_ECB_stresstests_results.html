VTB Bank (Austria) AG comments on the results of ECB’s stress tests

Vienna, 14th November 2015 - VTB Bank (Austria) AG as Head of European Subholding, incl. VTB Bank (France) SA and VTB Bank (Deutschland) AG, has passed the European Central Bank’s (ECB) stress tests, taking into consideration the capital increase effected in July 2015.

The ECB’s stress-tests assume a three-year model (2015-17) with two stress scenarios – basic and adverse. The starting point is the balance sheet as of the end of 2014. VTB Bank (Austria) AG successful passed the basic stress test for all three years. In the adverse scenario, the bank achieved the capital requirements for the first two years of the model, with a small shortfall in the final year (2017), driven mainly by a change of the capital adequacy requirements. To meet these requirements, VTB Bank (Austria) AG boosted the capital, raising its consolidated capital ratio from 9.3% as of the end of 2014 to 13.7% as of the end of September 2015. As of now the bank is fully compliant with all ECB requirements, and no additional measures are required currently.

Although this audit was a long-lasting and indeed a comprehensive assessment we managed to complete it in full compliance with the planned schedule. We appreciate the professional and at the same time positive cooperation between VTB Bank (Austria) AG and ECB teams in the course of the entire exercise.


About VTB Bank (Austria) AG

VTB Bank (Austria) AG is one of the largest foreign banks in Austria. The European Subholding of Russian VTB Bank is a commercial bank with EUR 9.6 billion in consolidated total assets and EUR 1.47 billion in eligible own funds. VTB Bank (Austria) AG is specialised in the financing of trade transactions and projects between East and West, as well as in services in the areas of payments and investments. The Austrian operations as well as the subsidiaries VTB Bank (Deutschland) AG and VTB Bank (France) SA are combined under VTB Bank (Austria) AG. VTB Direktbank, an online bank active in Germany, offers top terms for overnight and fixed term deposit accounts, is a branch office of VTB Bank (Austria) AG.
